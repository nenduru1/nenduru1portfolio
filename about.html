About

I am a PhD Candidate in Epidemiology and Trainee in Biomedical Informatics at the University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston. My research focuses on Alzheimer’s disease genetics, statistical genomics, and multi-omic data integration.

I am creating computational and statistical research pipelines that integrate genome-wide association studies, QTL data, single-nucleus RNA-seq, proteomic data, and multi-omic datasets to identify disease-associated genes, pathways, cell types, and molecular mediators.

My work combines statistical genomics, biomedical informatics, and machine learning to support gene prioritization and biological discovery in Alzheimer’s disease, neurodegenerative disorders, and immune-mediated diseases.

Research Interests

  • Alzheimer’s disease genetics: Understanding genetic risk, shared disease architecture, and molecular mechanisms involved in Alzheimer’s disease.
  • Statistical genomics: Applying GWAS, cross-trait meta-analysis, QTL integration, colocalization, Mendelian randomization, TWAS, and polygenic score analysis.
  • Multi-omic data integration: Integrating transcriptomic, proteomic, single-nucleus, and regulatory data to identify causal genes and disease-relevant pathways.
  • Immune-mediated and neurodegenerative diseases: Studying shared genetic and biological mechanisms between Alzheimer’s disease, frailty, and immune-mediated diseases.
  • Machine learning for biomedical discovery: Developing computational approaches for gene prioritization, pathway discovery, and disease mechanism interpretation.
